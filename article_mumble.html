Detecting Mumble Rap Using Data Science

Building a model using data science to objectively label rappers as mumblers.

Introduction

Those who know me, know that I spend a lot of time working with data and listening to rap music. Since receiving my first cd “Big Willie Style” by Will Smith in primary school, I have been listening to rap practically every day for multiple hours. From primary school till now, I acquired knowledge and experience in many fields including data and statistics, but of course the world of rap kept evolving too. In the recent history of hip hop a new subgenre known as “mumble rap” has emerged. Urban dictionary defines mumble rap as “A form of music that sounds like a stroke victim trying to deliver a speech; it is often incomprehensible and illogical.”. In my opinion, a good example of mumble rap would be Playboi Carti’s @ MEH, of which the top YouTube comment reads “Sounds good, can’t wait for the English version.”. However, not every example is as clear, and some rappers do not want to be associated with the term “mumble” at all. So who gets to decide which rappers meet the criteria for being a mumble rapper? Since even the Wikipedia entry describes it as “loosely defined”, I decided to open my data toolbox to get a more objective indication on how much your favorite rapper is mumbling.
